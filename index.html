Interface and interpretation

Graphesis provides a descriptive critical language for the analysis of graphical knowledge. In an interdisciplinary study fusing digital humanities with media studies and graphic design history, Johanna Drucker outlines the principles by which visual formats organize meaningful content. Among the most significant of these formats is the graphical user interface, the dominant feature of the screens of nearly all consumer electronic devices. Because so much of our personal and professional lives is mediated through visual interfaces, we saw the importance to start thinking critically about how they shape knowledge, our behavior, and even our identity. ‘The ability to think in and with the tools of computational and digital environments, will only evolve as quickly as our ability to articulate the metalanguages of our engagement’. — Johanna Drucker
